Transparency

A one-click transparency guide for Petz photos.

This guide was created for Windows.

Introduction

I'm tired of converting Petz photos using image editing software just to make them transparent for websites, and I'm sure you are, too.

What if I told you that you could convert an entire folder of Petz photos into transparent PNGs with a tap of your keyboard?

Step 1: Download Tools
  • PNG Background Transparency Converter
  • Chroma Key Wallpapers
Step 2: Install Tools

First, extract the PNG Background Transparency Converter archive anywhere onto your computer. Next, extract one or more chroma key wallpapers to the Wallpapers folder inside your Petz Resource directory.

Note 1: Despite being called a BMP to PNG converter, the executable is also capable of converting GIFs and PNGs into transparent PNGs. Feel free to save your petz photos as any PetzA-supported format.

Note 2: Chroma key wallpapers should map to Petz chart colors 249, 250, 251, 252, 253, and 254. Anything using these colors, including petz and accessories, will be made transparent. The default Plain (gray) wallpaper provided with the base game should also work as a chroma key. When prepping the Playpen, just be sure to choose the right chroma key for your petz.

Step 3: Settings

Once your chroma key wallpapers are installed, launch Petz and select: Options > General Options

At the General Options screen, find the Camera Options section and enable Include Background In Photos. Auto save is optional.

Step 4: Setup Playpen

Visit the Playpen playscene and select: Options > General Options

At the General Options screen, find the Playscene Wallpaper section and scroll through the wallpapers until you find one of the chroma key wallpapers. You may also use the Plain wallpaper. Wallpapers are sorted alphabetically.

Step 5: Take Photos

Once a chroma key wallpaper is applied, pick a petz and start taking photos.

Please note that the camera behaves differently when Include Background In Photos is enabled. The carrying case will appear in your photos if your petz are nearby, and photos will be cropped if your petz are partially off the screen for any reason.

Step 6: Move Photos

After you've taken your photos, open the PetzPix folder in your Petz game directory.

Select the photos you want to make transparent from the PetzPix folder and put them into the Graphics folder, located inside the PNG Background Transparency Converter directory you extracted earlier.

The converter will allow you to process multiple chroma key backgrounds at the same time, so you don't have to divide the files and process them separately.

Step 7: Execute Converter

Once you've put your photos into the Graphics folder, go back a directory and launch the executable file.

When the terminal window opens, it'll prompt you to press any key on your keyboard to run the conversion process. Press ENTER. The terminal window will close automatically once the process is complete.

Step 8: Check Photos

Once the terminal window closes, open the Converted folder, located inside the PNG Background Transparency Converter directory.

All photos from the Graphics folder should have been successfully converted and copied into the Converted folder. If all steps were completed correctly, only the backgrounds should be transparent, and eyes should be solid white, even if their outlines are missing black pixels.

Step 9: Cleanup

Once you've finished using the converter, don't forget to delete all unneeded photos from the Graphics folder. This will keep the working directory clean for new batches.

Optional: Petz Pic Factory

The PNG Background Transparency Converter also works great with Sherlock Software's Petz Pic Factory, an application used to extract adoption center photos (or pick-a-pet-menu photos) from .pet files.

If you'd like to use these photos in your projects, simply place the photos extracted from Petz Pic Factory into the Graphics folder, located inside the PNG Background Transparency Converter directory, and convert normally. Petz Pic Factory photos already have a chroma key background, so no extra work is required.

Hope it helps!

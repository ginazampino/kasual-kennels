Transparency

A one-click transparency guide for Petz photos.

Introduction

I'm tired of editing Petz photos using image editing software just to make them transparent for websites, and I'm sure you are, too.

What if I told you that you could convert an entire folder of Petz photos into transparent PNGs with a tap of your keyboard?

Step 1: Download Tools
  • PNG Background Transparency Converter
  • Chroma Key Wallpapers
Step 2: Install Tools

First, extract the PNG Background Transparency Converter archive anywhere onto your system.

Next, extract one or more chroma key wallpapers to your Wallpapers folder inside your Petz Resource directory.

Note 1: Despite being called a BMP to PNG converter, the executable is also capable of converting GIFs and PNGs into transparent PNGs. Feel free to use whichever format(s) you prefer.

Note 2: Chroma key wallpapers map to Petz chart colors 249, 250, 251, 252, 253, and 254. Anything using these colors, including petz and accessories, will be made transparent. Be sure to choose the right chroma key for your needs.

Step 3: Settings

Once your chroma key wallpapers are installed, launch Petz and select: Options > General Options

At the General Options screen, find the Camera Options section and enable Include Background In Photos. Auto save is optional.

Step 4: Setup Playpen

Visit the Playpen playscene and select: Options > General Options

At the General Options screen, find the Playscene Wallpaper section and scroll through the wallpapers until you find one of the chroma key wallpapers you installed earlier. Wallpapers are sorted alphabetically.

Step 5: Take Photos

Once a chroma key wallpaper is applied, pick a petz and start taking photos.

You may experience some lag between photos, so this technique may not be suitable for show photos, but normally you'd want solid, unedited white backgrounds for those, anyway.

Step 6: Move Photos

After you've taken your photos, open the PetzPix folder in your Petz game directory.

Select the photos you want to make transparent from the PetzPix folder and move or copy them into the Graphics folder inside the PNG Background Transparency Converter directory you extracted earlier.

Step 7: Execute Converter

Once you've moved or copied your photos into the Graphics folder, go back a directory and launch the executable file.

When the terminal window opens, it'll prompt you to press any key on your keyboard to run the conversion process. Press ENTER. The terminal window will close automatically once the process is complete.

Step 8: Check Photos

Once the terminal window closes, open the Converted folder inside the PNG Background Transparency Converter directory.

All photos from the Graphics folder should have been successfully converted and copied into the Converted folder. If all steps were completed correctly, only the backgrounds should be transparent. When converting dogz, the whites of their eyes should remain solid.

Step 9: Cleanup

Once you've finished using the converter, don't forget to delete all unneeded photos from the Graphics folder. This will keep the working directory clean for new batches.

Optional: Petz Pic Factory

The PNG Background Transparency Converter also works great with Sherlock Software's Petz Pic Factory, an application used to extract adoption center photos (or pick-a-pet-menu photos) from .pet files.

If you'd like to use these photos in your projects, simply place the photos extracted from Petz Pic Factory into the Graphics folder inside the PNG Background Transparency Converter directory and convert normally. Petz Pic Factory photos are extracted with a chroma key background (magenta) already applied, so no extra work is required.

Hope it helps!
